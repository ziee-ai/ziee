# HUMAN_FEEDBACK — ui-batch

Verbatim human feedback on this work, with how each was addressed. Recorded at
the moment it was given (all three arrived on the plan, before implementation).

- **FB-1** [status: resolved] — *"NOTE 1 (Fix 1 model selector): make it DYNAMIC instead of a fixed cap - always show the full model name whenever there is room, ellipsis only under real pressure. Drop the fixed max-w entirely; let the trigger size to its content so GPT OSS 120B and most names show in full in the normal wide composer with NO ellipsis; keep min-w-0 plus truncate on the label so it can still shrink; place the selector in a flex context that yields BEFORE the send button (send stays shrink-0, the model name gives way first). Add a soft ceiling (cap around 40-50 percent of the composer width, or a generous max-w) so one pathological name cannot swallow the toolbar. Accepted tradeoff: the toolbar reflows when switching between short and long model names - that is fine, we prefer fit over stable width. Verify in the gallery at BOTH a wide composer AND a narrow split pane."* → The plan's fixed 176px cap was replaced with content sizing: the fixed `max-w-[130px]` is gone, `shrink-0` moved from the toolbar's right group onto the Send button alone, and the model slot got `min-w-0`, so the name yields first and Send cannot. Two bounds implement the "soft ceiling" — `max-w-[20rem]` absolute on the trigger, and `max-w-[60%]` of the toolbar row on the right group (the latter added during implementation after measuring that flex shrink alone starved the LEFT group to 2px at 390px and its "+" button overflowed). Verified in the gallery at both widths, in both themes, by TEST-2…TEST-5. Two implementation corrections came out of following this literally and measuring rather than reasoning: `w-auto` is wrong on a `<button>` (form controls size shrink-to-fit, so it ignored available space and overflowed its container), and a name that FITS never ellipsizes at any real viewport — so a second, over-long gallery seed was needed to exercise the ellipsis path at all. [generalizable: yes — size a variable-length label to its CONTENT with a relative bound (a % of a definite-width ancestor) rather than a fixed pixel cap, and put `shrink-0` on the one control that must never yield rather than on the whole group; then prove BOTH regimes, since a fix that only ever truncates and a fix that never truncates both look correct in a single screenshot]
- **FB-2** [status: resolved] — *"NOTE 2 (Fix 2 sidebar titles): before committing to dropping the kit Menu group wrapper plus regenerating the testid/coverage/state-matrix registries, confirm the kit Menu group has NO title-className or label-className seam that would let you reduce the title inset in place with far less churn - if such a seam exists, prefer it; if not, proceed with the group-drop, but verify the a11y gate confirms it does not lose the title-to-items association (each nav keeps an aria-label covering its items)."* → Checked before proceeding: the full `MenuProps` + `MenuItem` union in `sdk/packages/kit/src/kit/menu.tsx` exposes no `titleClassName`, no `labelClassName` and no per-item `className`; the group title is a hardcoded `<div className="px-3 py-1 text-xs font-medium text-muted-foreground">` at `menu.tsx:174`, and `MenuProps` reaches only the `<ul>` (via `className`) and the `<nav>` (via `style`). The one in-place lever — `label` is `React.ReactNode`, so `<span className="-ms-2">` would cancel the inset — was rejected and the reasoning recorded (DEC-5): it hardcodes the Menu's own internal `px-2` and leaves the typography drift unfixed. Proceeded with the group-drop. On a11y: each `<nav>` keeps its `aria-label` ("Primary navigation" / "Tools navigation"), which is the landmark naming the section and covering its items, and the kit itself documents the group caption as decorative chrome; `gate:ui`'s axe pass reports no a11y finding on the sidebar. The churn concern also proved smaller than feared — the group testids are derived at runtime inside the kit rather than being source literals, and `src/components/common/` is outside the gallery's `surfaceRoots`, so no coverage entry was needed. [generalizable: yes — before restructuring app code around a third-party/kit component's styling, read that component's full prop surface for an existing seam, and record why a rejected seam was rejected; and prefer moving a caption OUT of a component you cannot style over smuggling a negative margin that encodes that component's internal padding]
- **FB-3** [status: resolved] — *"NOTE 3 (Fix 3 split collapse): CONFIRMED - we accept the deliberate side effect that browser-Back after New Chat will not resurrect the old split; New Chat means a fresh single-pane surface, proceed as planned."* → Implemented as planned: `Stores.SplitView.reset()` in `NewChatPage`'s mount effect. The side effect is recorded as an accepted tradeoff in DEC-6 and in the commit message, so a future reader meets it as a decision rather than a bug. The paired boundary — creating a chat from INSIDE a split pane must still adopt into that pane — is pinned by the existing `new-chat-adopt.spec.ts`, run as a control alongside the new collapse spec.

## Awaiting human review

The three fixes have not yet been reviewed by a human against the running app.
Two items need the human's decision and are the reason this is not yet a clean
9/9 — both are **pre-existing repo/environment conditions, not defects in this
diff**, and both are evidenced in TEST_RESULTS.md:

1. **The full-stack e2e suite cannot run in this environment** — its harness
   spins up a per-run Postgres container, and this session's user is not in the
   `docker` group (`permission denied … unix:///var/run/docker.sock`, at the very
   first step of `global-setup`). TEST-6…TEST-9 are therefore written and
   statically verified but UNRUN. The gallery-driven specs (TEST-1…TEST-5) do run
   and pass.
2. **`npm run check` is red on the base commit** — five gates
   (`check:testid-registry`, `check:gallery-coverage`, `check:state-matrix`,
   `check:overlay-registry`, `check:override-registry`) fail identically on a
   clean, untouched checkout of `khoi`. They stem from the kit's extraction into
   the `sdk` submodule, whose generated artifacts were never refreshed. Fixing
   them is a separate piece of work — partly in another repository — and is
   deliberately not bundled into this PR.
